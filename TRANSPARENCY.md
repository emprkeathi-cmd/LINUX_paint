# How to get a transparent background (the checkerboard)

In Windows 11 Paint, the gray/white checker grid means "transparent —
nothing here." This version now does the same thing.

## Steps
1. Press **Ctrl+L** to open the Layers panel on the right.
2. At the BOTTOM of that panel there's a "Canvas" swatch that says
   **Background: ON**.
3. Click it. It flips to **Background: OFF (transparent)** and the white
   canvas behind your image turns into the checkerboard grid.
4. Now any empty area — anywhere your layers don't have paint — is truly
   transparent.
5. The eraser also erases to transparent, so you can rub out parts of the
   image and the checkerboard shows through.

## Saving it
Save with **Ctrl+S** and choose a **.png** filename. PNG keeps
transparency. (JPG/BMP can't store transparency, so those will fill the
see-through areas with the background colour instead.)

## Tip
If you open an image that doesn't fill the whole canvas and you want the
empty surround gone, just turn Background OFF and save as PNG — the
surround becomes transparent.
